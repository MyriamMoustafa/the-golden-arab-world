THE TWO MOST FAMOUSE FOODS IN MOROCCO:


1- Couscous



( The historical story)


Ambouaza points out that “couscous was found among the indigenous people of North Africa before it was divided into countries, where it was found in Egypt, Mauritania, Libya, Tunisia, Morocco, and Algeria...it spread over time throughout the world thanks to the Amazigh travelers.” Cooking utensils similar to those used in preparing couscous were found in tombs dating back to the period of King Masinissa (238 BC - 148 BC).

(Ingredients)


2 kilograms of couscous
1/2 kilogram chicken, washed and drained
6 carrots, peeled and cut lengthwise
5 turnips
5 pumpkins, green
3 pumpkins, red
1 cabbage, medium size, cut into four pieces
3 potatoes
2 tomatoes
1 eggplant, to taste
1 onion, cut into small pieces
2 cups chickpeas, soaked the night before
1 bunch of coriander
1 bunch of parsley
1/2 teaspoon black pepper
1/2 teaspoon turmeric
1/2 teaspoon ginger
A pinch of cinnamon
1/4 teaspoon cumin
1/2 teaspoon ghee, unsalted (or a cube to taste of ghee)

2-Harira soup

(The historical story )


Some historians trace the origin of harira to Andalusia.
Harira soup is a delicious dish that the Maghreb countries are famous for. It is a complete meal that is usually served with dates and Moroccan chebakia in Morocco, and in Algeria it is served with burak or maqouda.

(Ingredients)


A cup of chickpeas
- A cup of meat pieces
- Two onions
- Two tomatoes
- Two tablespoons of butter
- Two tablespoons of vermicelli
- Two tablespoons of flour
- Two tablespoons of tomato sauce
- A liter and a half of water
- Half a teaspoon of saffron
- A teaspoon of salt
- A teaspoon of black pepper
- Two tablespoons of coriander
- Two tablespoons of parsley
