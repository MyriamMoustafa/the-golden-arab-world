THE TWO MOST FAMOUSE FOODS IN PALESTINE:

1-Musakhan


( The historical story)


Musakhan is considered one of the most popular Palestinian meals that is prepared throughout the year, but it is famous during the olive harvest season. The musakhan dish is considered a model of the identity of ancient Palestinian cuisine and its formation over time, as it is an ancient dish linked to the cultural heritage of society. Since ancient times, farmers in Palestine have been working to grow olives, and harvest time is considered a joyful holiday that they rush to celebrate, especially at the time of making olive oil, and from here it appeared. A meal that relies heavily on olive oil.

contains proteins, carbohydrates, dietary fiber and unsaturated fats,

(Ingredients)


Whole chicken, cleaned and cut into quarters.
A kilogram of onions chopped into slices.
A cup of olive oil.
Two loaves of large Arabic bread.
Sufficient amount of sumac.
Sufficient amount of fried pine nuts for decoration.
A spoonful of salt.
A spoonful of black pepper.



2- Maqluba





(The historical story)


Palestine was the first country to know maqluba, and they called it eggplant because of the use of eggplant as a basic ingredient in it. The story says that when the leader Saladin al-Ayyubi conquered the city of Jerusalem, people celebrated this victory and presented the soldiers with a dish of eggplant, which Saladin liked very much. When he asked them about its name, they described it as maqluba, so he called it this. the name.
The second narration indicates that the origins of the maqlouba dish go back to the Abbasid era, especially since different recipes for making this dish were mentioned in the two most important cookbooks published in that period, which are the book “Cooking and Food Reform, Foods and Delicacies of Prepared Foods from What Were Extracted from Medical Books and the Words of Chefs and People of Knowledge” by Ibn Sayyar Al-Warraq, and the second book is “Al-Tabikh” by Al-Baghdadi, which is considered one of the oldest Arabic cookery books, and was written between 940 and 960 AD.
